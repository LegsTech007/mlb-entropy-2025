What is Entropy?

In information theory, entropy measures unpredictability in outcomes. For baseball, we treat each plate appearance outcome (HR, K, BB, 1B, etc.) as a category and compute how spread out those outcomes are for a player or team.

The formula (in natural units, “nats”) is:
H = - Σ (pᵢ · ln pᵢ), where pᵢ is the frequency of each outcome.

  • High entropy: many different outcomes with similar frequency → less predictable.
  • Low entropy: outcomes concentrated in a few types → more predictable.
