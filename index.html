What is Entropy?

In information theory, entropy measures the uncertainty or unpredictability in a set of outcomes. For baseball, we treat each plate appearance outcome (HR, K, BB, 1B, etc.) as a category.

Entropy is computed as:
H = - Σ (pᵢ * log(pᵢ))
where pᵢ is the probability of each outcome.

- High entropy → the player produces a wide mix of outcomes, making them less predictable.
- Low entropy → the player’s outcomes are concentrated (e.g., mostly strikeouts).
